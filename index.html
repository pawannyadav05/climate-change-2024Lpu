Understanding the effects of climate change.

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant, long-term shifts in Earth’s average weather patterns, especially an increase in global temperatures. While Earth’s climate has naturally fluctuated over millions of years, recent changes are occurring at an unprecedented rate, largely due to human activities. The primary driver of modern climate change is the release of greenhouse gases, including carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O), into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, creating a “greenhouse effect” that warms the planet.

Over the past century, global temperatures have risen significantly, leading to severe environmental consequences.

Causes of Climate Change

  • Greenhouse Gas Emissions: Burning fossil fuels for energy releases carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases.
  • Deforestation: Cutting down trees reduces carbon absorption, increasing atmospheric CO2 levels.
  • Agriculture: Livestock and fertilizers produce methane and nitrous oxide, potent greenhouse gases.
  • Industrial Processes: Manufacturing activities emit various greenhouse gases during production.
  • Waste Management: Decomposing organic waste in landfills produces methane, contributing to climate change

These activities increase the concentration of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, which trap heat and lead to a rise in global temperatures.

How Can We Prevent It?

  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle materials to minimize waste.
  • Switch to renewable energy sources (solar, wind, hydroelectric).
  • Increase energy efficiency in homes and industries (use energy-saving appliances).
  • Promote sustainable transportation (walking, cycling, public transport).
  • Support reforestation and sustainable agriculture practices.

Collective efforts in these areas can significantly reduce our carbon footprint and help mitigate the effects of climate change.

Observed Warming and its Causes

  1. Global surface temperature
    • Global Temperature Rise: The global surface temperature was 1.09°C higher in 2011–2020 compared to 1850–1900, with an increase of 1.59°C over land and 0.88°C over the ocean.

    • Early 21st Century Warming: Between 2001 and 2020, global surface temperature was 0.99°C higher than the baseline period of 1850–1900.

    • Accelerated Warming Since 1970: The rate of global temperature increase has been faster since 1970 than in any other 50-year period over at least the last 2000 years (high confidence).

  2. Human-Caused Warming
    • From 1850–1900 to 2010–2019, human activities likely raised global surface temperature by 0.8°C to 1.3°C, with a best estimate of 1.07°C.

    • During this period, greenhouse gases contributed a warming of 1.0°C to 2.0°C, aerosols caused cooling of 0.0°C to 0.8°C, natural factors (solar and volcanic) changed temperature by –0.1°C to +0.1°C, and internal variability affected it by –0.2°C to +0.2°C.

Daily Activities that Contribute to CO₂ Emission

  • Driving Cars: Burning gasoline or diesel for transportation releases CO₂.
  • Using Electricity: Powering lights, appliances, and electronics often relies on fossil fuels.
  • Heating and Cooling Homes: Using heaters and air conditioners consumes energy, especially if derived from fossil fuels.
  • Eating Meat and Dairy: Meat and dairy production, especially from cattle, emits significant CO₂ and methane.
  • Buying Products: Manufacturing, packaging, and transporting goods release CO₂.
  • Flying: Air travel has a large carbon footprint per passenger mile.
  • Using Plastic: The production, disposal, and sometimes incineration of plastic generates CO₂.
  • Wasting Food: Decomposing food waste in landfills produces methane, and energy is wasted in its production and transport.
  • Internet Usage: Data centers powering online activities consume significant energy, contributing to CO₂ emissions.
  • Laundry and Dishwashing: Heating water and running machines require energy, especially if not from renewable sources.

Key Effects of Climate Change

  • Rising Temperatures:

    - Global temperatures rise, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves.

    - Hotter climates disrupt ecosystems and impact human health.

  • Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels:

    - Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels.

    - Coastal areas face increased risks of flooding and erosion.

  • Extreme Weather Events:

    - Climate change leads to more severe hurricanes, droughts, and wildfires.

    - These events damage infrastructure and disrupt communities.

  • Ecosystem and Biodiversity Loss:

    - Species unable to adapt to rapid changes face extinction risks.

    - Loss of biodiversity weakens ecosystems and natural processes.

  • Ocean Warming and Acidification:

    - Warmer and more acidic oceans harm coral reefs and marine life.

    - Disruption in marine food chains affects biodiversity and fisheries.
